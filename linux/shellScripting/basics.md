All of the following characters have a special meaning or function. If they are
used in a way that their special meaning isn't needed, they must be *escaped*.

To escape a character, use a backslash (`\`).

```bash
\ / ; , . ~ # $ ? & * ( ) [ ] ‘ ’ " + - ! ^ = | < >
```
